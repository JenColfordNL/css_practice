Summary:

The tiny, yet mighty, hedgehog (Erinaceus europaeus) is a delightful and quirky little creature who is loved both as a pet and as an addition to many European gardens. The hedgehog is a sensitive creature who is both predator and prey in the wild and leads a very sheltered domestic life.

The hedgehog is a mammal whose lifespan averages 2-3 years in the wild and 3-6 years as a pet. Their unusual and striking appearenece has made them trendy pets in recent years. Hedgehog have a coat of spikiy quills on their back. These spikes act as a defensive tactic. When these tiny mammals sense danger they curl up into a spiky ball, much like the position they take when they hibernate. Hibernation: The dormant sleep-like state animals adopt to conseve energy during the winter months.

Hedheogs' average weight varies by region but they average aproximately 2kg. Their tiny size makes them an ideal pet for urban pet owners as they require minimal space for exercise and play. Domestic hedgehogs enjoy a diet of specially formulated pet food as well as insects provided by the pet owner. Pet hedgehogs also enjoy a climate-controlled life as concientious pet owners ensure their home is always at least 22 degresss celsius.

Not unlike domestic hedgehogs, wild hedgehogs are often celebrated for their ability to elimate pests in residential gardens. Wild hedgehogs used to be classified by the no longer recognized order: Insectivoria. Hedgehogs, however, are omnivores. They thrive on a diet of worms, snakes, insects, toads, berries, grossroots and mushrooms. In the wild, a hedgehogs lifespan is much shorter. The average wild hedgehog's lifespan is 2-3 years.

Hedgehogs are delightful pets and widely appraciated as helpers in the urban wild. Their gentle demeanor means they pose little danger to humans. As hedgehogs have no natural predator's in countries like New Zealand and The Scottish Isles, they have upset the balance of the ecosystem in these places.
